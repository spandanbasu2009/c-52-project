Interesting Facts About A Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting Facts About A Chameleon

  • Chameleons mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  • Chameleons vary wildly in terms of size.
  • Unlike many lizards, chameleons cannot regrow their tails.

Interesting Facts About A Cicada

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  • Cicadas inundate forests as a survival mechanism.
  • The lengthy life cycles of cicadas may help them evade predators.

Interesting Facts About A Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.

Interesting Facts About A Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Interesting Facts About A Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears are generally used as kids' soft toys
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed teddy bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
